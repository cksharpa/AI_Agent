Selected Company: **HCL Technologies Limited**

**Rationale**  
- **Undervalued Relative to Peers:** PE = 46.2 and EV/EBITDA = 21.8, considerably below the median peer levels (PE ≈ 60, EV/EBITDA ≈ 27).  
- **Exceptional Margin Profile & Cash Flow:** 41.5% EBITDA margin and low debt (0.12× EBITDA) provide a robust balance sheet and strong cash‑generation capacity.  
- **High‑Growth AI & Automation Driver:** 35% of new revenue is expected from SmartOps & AI, and a new AI platform (HCL Applied AI) targets 80% of engineering projects by 2026, positioning the company well in the growing AI/automation market.  
- **Strategic Client Wins & M&A Platform:** $900 M contract with JPMorgan Chase and an active M&A strategy (L3 Partners) expand both revenue base and service depth.  
- **Risk Profile:** Moderately affected by cloud‑operations dependency and a small USD revenue mix (~12%), but overall risk is outweighed by the high margin and growth prospects.  
- **Investment Signal:** “Buy” recommendation with a target of ₹1,900, suggesting substantial upside.

**Other Companies – Reasons for Rejection**  

- **Infosys Limited**  
  - *Valuation*: PE = 68.5 and EV/EBITDA = 30.2, substantially above peer medians, indicating a valuation premium.  
  - *Risk*: Significant talent churn risk and competitive pressure in cloud services.  
  - *Return Outlook*: Growth 12–14% capped by high valuation and modest margin improvement expectations.  
  - *Result*: While growth prospects are solid, the premium price reduces potential return, leading to a lower preference than HCL.

- **Tata Consultancy Services (TCS)**  
  - *Valuation*: PE = 54.8 and EV/EBITDA = 26.5 are closer to peers but still higher than HCL’s.  
  - *Risk*: Margin compression due to commoditisation and talent shortages, coupled with geopolitical trade headwinds.  
  - *Return Outlook*: Presented as “Hold” with a target of ₹23,700; upside is limited relative to the large market share and mature business.  
  - *Result*: The valuation premium, coupled with a hold recommendation and moderate upside, makes TCS less attractive compared to HCL’s undervalued status and higher margin trajectory.
